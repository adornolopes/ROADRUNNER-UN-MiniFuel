## Overview

The ROADRUNNER campaign is the **first systematic application of the MiniFuel irradiation platform to uranium nitride (UN) fuel** at Oak Ridge National Laboratory's (ORNL) High Flux Isotope Reactor (HFIR). This experiment is designed to generate high-quality irradiation performance data for UN fuel under tightly controlled and largely isothermal conditions, addressing critical gaps in the historical irradiation database.

### Key Features

- **36 UN minidisc specimens** with systematically varied fabrication parameters
- **Three temperature regimes**: 873 K, 1,173 K, and 1,473 K
- **Three burnup levels**: ~3.75%, ~6.0%, and ~7.5% FIMA
- **Accelerated burnup**: ~9Ã— faster than commercial PWRs using HFIR's removable beryllium (RB) reflector positions
- **Controlled variables**: as-fabricated density (86â€“96% TD), carbon impurity (961â€“5,240 ppm), oxygen content (â‰¤~2,000 ppm), and grain size (2.5â€“24 Âµm)

### Campaign Objectives

1. Generate discriminating FGR and swelling data to resolve divergence among existing empirical correlations
2. Isolate effects of temperature, burnup, density, and impurity content on UN fuel performance
3. Provide calibration data for physics-based fuel performance models (e.g., UNSiFGRS in BISON)
4. Reassess historical fuel specification limits for industrial-scale UN fabrication

## FGR Correlations

### Storms Correlation
Function of temperature *T* (K), burnup *BU* (%FIMA), and density *Ï* (%TD):

$$\text{FGR} = \frac{100}{\exp\left[0.0025 \times \left(90 \times \frac{\rho^{0.77}}{BU^{0.09}} - T\right)\right] + 1}$$

### Rogozkin Correlation
Function of temperature *T* (Â°C) and burnup *BU* (%FIMA):

$$\text{FGR} = 3.05 \times BU^{1.92} \times \exp\left(\frac{-2086}{T}\right)$$
